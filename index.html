About Us

Startups2Students is an event that brings together high-quality, self-motivated students and startups. Students everywhere want to get involved with startups and learn from entrepreneurs that are currently executing on their startup dreams. Startups2Students reverses the dynamic of recruitment by bringing local startup companies to a venue where they can give a pitch to the students, removing the resume first approach.

What is Our Goal?

The goal is to foster relationships between professionals and students willing to get involved and add value to a vision. It’s open-ended, so students can get involved via internships, jobs, innovative projects, and/or build productive friendships. Whatever the case is, when you create an environment that facilitates relationships between high-impact oriented people and startups, anything is possible!

The Startups2Students event features the following activities:

  • Startups give a 2 minute pitch to the students
  • Startups talk directly with interested students
  • No paper resume exchanges!

Event FAQs

What is the cost of admission?

Startups2Students is a free event, both for the students and the startups

What type of students will be attending?

All types of students are welcome to attend and Startups2Students is advertised as an event for all students. That being said, most of our advertising outreach will be to tech & business students

How do I get involved as a startup company?

Please use the Get Involved section above to give us your contact information and we will be in touch with you shortly thereafter with details on how you can be a part of Startups2Students

What is the format of the pitch to students?

The pitch is a brief 2 minute pitch directed from startups directly to the students. You can use powerpoint in your presentation, but know that you will be limited to 2 minutes. The goal of the pitch is to tell students about your startup and give students the reason why your company is great to work for.

Why aren't resumes allowed?

Usually at career fairs companies collect large stacks of resumes and many students never get contacted. This is a waste of time for both the business and the student. The goal of this event is to network with the startups community, make connections, and only exchange resumes if you hit it off. You may bring calling/business cards with contact information to exchange.

How should I dress?

It will be a relaxed, casual environment, no need to dress up.
